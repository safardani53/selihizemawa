London Waterloo station in rush hour chaos as South Western Railway trains cancelled and delayed

IRATE passengers have remained furious with South Western Railway today as the travel hell of this morning continued into the evening rush.

South Western Railway told passengers not to travel this morning as none of its trains were running between Surbiton and London Waterloo, the UK's busiest railway station.

Woking, Surbiton, Raynes Park and Earlsfield stations were all closed and there was major disruption at Wimbledon and Clapham Junction stations.

Southern services between East Croydon and London Victoria were also affected after Network Rail engineering works overran in the Hampton Court area.

And the chaos continued into this evening with irate passengers taking to Twitter to vent their frustration.

Kelly Peacock tweeted: "#SouthWesternRailway your service has been shocking ALL day! Thanks a bunch."

Katy added: "All these #SouthWesternRailway cancellations! Since this morning! And now I'm on a bus instead of a train that I had to pay for. At least give us a replacement service!"

Clair Spence added succinctly: "South Western railways you suck."

On Monday morning normally-packed commuter trains were cancelled, leaving tens of thousands of passengers stranded.

Amid the travel chaos, South Western Railway tweeted to remind commuters about planned strike action this Saturday.

Responding to that, irritated passengers slammed the firm for its "insane" timing.

Caz Blacklock-Vandy said: "This could have waited until either later today or tomorrow. There are currently people stranded all over the south of England unable to get to work or important appointments. Have a heart".

Julian Shea tweeted: "The secret of comedy is timing".

Fran Murphy wrote on Twitter: "Seriously timing isn't your forte is it? You have caused chaos today with no trains and decided to share this!"

The biggest rail workers' union today called on Transport Secretary Chris Grayling to resign because of the "Monday mayhem".

Furious commuters took to social media to complain about the disruption, describing the cancellations as "unacceptable" and "the definition of a total and utter shambles."

Billie Harrison tweeted: "@SW_Railway In case any of you actually care about your travellers, I have a huge tax exam today in Weybridge at 10, EVERY TRAIN IS CANCELLED UNTIL 10."

Tooting resident Kat Storr said: "The impact this must be having on thousands of people's working days is ridiculous."

Steve Brine Tory MP for Winchester said: "I am dismayed, as an MP and a commuter and have asked @SW_Railway and Network Rao; to get a report and apology on my desk today. This is a totally unacceptable situation. I will also be speaking with the Secretary of State today, when/if I can get to London."

Another angry traveller tweeted: "'Commuters have been told not to travel' Are you joking? We can't all put our lives in hold due to your incompetence, if we don't turn up, we don't get paid".

Irate travellers complained about a lack of communication after they were left shivering on freezing train platforms with no replacement bus service to go on.

Disgruntled commuter Kenneth Conway wrote on Twitter: "I have had to walk in the freezing cold from Motspur Park to Wimbledon because there were no trains and no rail replacement buses. This is shambolic and I would query what I am getting for my money."

Ilane Lassman said: "Is it acceptable to keep passengers hanging on to the last minute before cancelling all trains from #Waterloo without explanation? Hundreds now unable to get to work".

Today's travel chaos was the latest disruption to hit SWR following a spate of problems involving signal and points failures.

A long-running dispute over the role of guards on trains has also crippled services because of strikes.

SWR and Network Rail apologised for the disruption after they were inundated with tweets from angry commuters.

General secretary Mick Cash said: "The chaos into Waterloo this morning is just another example of the fragmentation and division on Britain's privatised railways where maintenance schedules and budgets are hacked to the bare bones while private operators like SWR are left with a free hand to rob the network blind in the name of profit.

"What we need is reintegration and a coherent system with all elements of the rail operation working together in one publicly-owned framework, with investment rather than exploitation as the watchword.

"While the crisis on our railways deepens, the Transport Secretary Chris Grayling is markedly absent while he ponders his self-interest and a possible resignation from the May Government. He should go, and he should go now."

Anthony Smith, chief executive of the independent watchdog Transport Focus, said: "Passengers rely on the railway - this morning tens of thousands of passengers coming into Waterloo could not.

"While engineering and improvement works need to be done, such late notice of the fundamental problems on the approaches to Waterloo this morning is unacceptable.

"A quick, thorough review of the problems uncovered in the engineering works and the passenger information put out should be carried out and published so that lessons can be learned.

"In the meantime, passengers should claim for compensation and make sure their voice is heard."
