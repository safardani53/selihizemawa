Who is Jim Sciutto's wife, Gloria Riviera?

ABC News correspondent, Gloria Riviera, is an award-winning journalist.

The on-air reporter is married to CNN chief national security correspondent Jim Sciutto.

Who is Jim Sciutto's wife, Gloria Riviera?

Gloria Riviera is an ABC News correspondent currently based in Washington DC.

She joined the broadcast network back in 1999 after graduating from New York University with a master’s degree in journalism, according to her LinkedIn profile.

Riviera has had quite a lengthy career with ABC News as a producer and on-air reporter.

She started off as the assistant to then senior White House Correspondent, 20/20, and This Week Anchor, Sam Donaldson.

In 2001, Riviera reported from ground zero as an associate producer for news coverage as both towers fell - which won her an Emmy for breaking news coverage of 9/11.

Riviera also won the Alfred I. DuPont Award in 2003 for her work on the special 9/11: Answering Children’s Questions. 

Her many roles included

  • Off-air reporter at the United Nations (2002)
  • Covered the 2004 Presidential Election while on ABC News’ political team
  • New York correspondent for ABC News’ affiliate news service, Newsone (2005)
  • Covered European and Middle Eastern news for ABC in London (2006)

When did Sciutto and Riviera get married?

Riviera was previously married to William Francis Hughes III, a New York investment firm banker.

The pair tied the knot in August 2002, but their union ended in divorce.

Riviera remarried in 2006 to CNN co-anchor, Sciutto.

The couple met for the first time while they were both in Baghdad on assignment, according to a Washingtonian profile published on Sciutto in 2014.

Riviera's ABC bio says she and her family are avid outdoor enthusiasts.

They enjoy hiking, skiing, and back-country camping.

How many children do Sciutto and Riviera have?

Sciutto and Riviera share three children together.

They have two sons, Tristan and Caden, and one daughter, Sinclair.
