Wife, Charity, &amp; Net Worth [2023 Update]

Raul Neto, a professional basketball player hailing from Brazil, currently plays as a point guard for the Cleveland Cavaliers (2022- Present).

Neto started his career in Brazil before making his way to Spain and eventually playing in the NBA.

Throughout his career in the NBA, he has played for multiple teams, including the Utah Jazz, Philadelphia 76ers, Washington Wizards, and the Cavaliers.

Apart from being a dedicated athlete, Neto is also a devoted family man. He is married to Janae Togni Neto and plays an active role in his stepson’s life.

Moreover, he co-founded the Happy Tooth Foundation along with Tyler Hanks, an organization that provides free dental care to underprivileged families around the world.

Raul Neto’s Family Details!

Raul Neto was born in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, on May 19, 1992, to parents Claudia and Raul Togni Filho.

His father, Raul Togni Filho (born on October 23, 1964), is a former Brazilian basketball player who attended Superior Complete for high school and married Claudio Rodrigues Leal Togni on June 18, 1988.

His father attended his high school at Superior Complete and married Claudio Rodrigues Leal Togni on June 18, 1988. 

Raul Neto is not an only child; he has two elder brothers. Diego Leal Togni, Raul’s brother, currently resides in Wellington, New Zealand.

Gabriel Togni, his other brother, attended Colegio Pitagoras Cidade Jardim for high school and studied computer information systems at Missouri Valley College.

He currently works as a programmer at the New Mexico Military Institute.

Before NBA Career

In the 2008-09 FIBA Americas League, Neto started his professional basketball career with Minas Tenis Clube.

After playing for three seasons with Minas, he joined Gipuzkoa in the Lagun Aro GBC league for the 2011-12 season.

On August 1, 2014, Neto signed a three-year agreement with UCAM Murcia. However, after the 2014-15 season, he left the team to sign with the NBA.

NBA Journey Of Raul Neto

On October 28, 2015, Neto made his NBA debut for the Utah Jazz against the Detroit Pistons, scoring 8 points.

He spent four seasons with the Utah Jazz from the 2015-16 season to the 2018-19 season, playing in 199 games and starting in 54, and averaged 4.8 points per game.

In the 2019-20 season, he joined the Philadelphia 76ers, playing in 54 games and starting 3, and averaged 5.1 points per game.

He then played for the Washington Wizards for two seasons from 2020-21 to 2021-22, participating in 134 games and starting 41, averaging 8.1 points per game.

On May 6, 2021, he recorded his career-high of 25 points while playing for the Washington Wizards against the Toronto Raptors, resulting in a thrilling 131-129 overtime win.

Neto is currently playing for the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2022-23 season.

National Team

Neto was part of the Brazil U-18 team that secured an impressive runner-up position at the 2010 FIBA Americas Under-18 championship.

He then became the youngest senior Brazil national basketball team player at the 2010 FIBA World Championship, although he had limited playing time.

Neto has represented the senior national team at the 2012 Summer Olympics, the 2013 FIBA AmeriCup, the 2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup, and the 2016 Summer Olympics.

Net Worth & Contracts

Net Worth

As of 2023, Raul Neto’s net worth is estimated to be around $5 million, which he earned primarily through his 15-year-long basketball career.

Besides basketball, he has also invested in two startup companies. One of them is Pablito, which he describes as an “Uber with minivans,” while the other is an investment platform for art called “Artopolie.”

Contracts

Currently, Raul Neto is under a one-year contract with the Cleveland Cavaliers, having signed a deal worth $2.46 million for the 2022-23 NBA season.

The Cavaliers signed him along with point guard Ricky Rubio and center Robin Lopez.

Neto’s NBA journey began in 2013 when the Atlanta Hawks drafted him as the 47th overall pick in the second round of the 2013 NBA Draft.

However, the Hawks traded him to the Utah Jazz on the same day.

On July 9, 2015, Neto signed a three-year contract worth $2.7 million with the Jazz.

He later signed a new two-year deal with the Jazz, worth $4.4 million, on July 6, 2018, but was waived on July 1, 2019, due to the Mike Conley trade with the Grizzlies.

On July 12, 2019, he signed a one-year contract worth $1.73 million with the Philadelphia 76ers.

He later joined the Washington Wizards, signing two one-year contracts worth $1.8 million and $2.08 million on November 21, 2020, and August 6, 2021, respectively.

Happy Tooth Foundation

The Happy Tooth Foundation was launched in 2022 by Raul Neto and Tyler Hanks, an Arizona School of Dentistry & Oral Health graduate. 

Their mission is to offer free dental care to underprivileged families and communities worldwide. 

To achieve this, they have teamed up with Quail, an application that encourages and facilitates long-term dental health improvement.

The foundation is sponsored and supported by the National Basketball Players Association (NBPA).
