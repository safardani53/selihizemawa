JAMIE Lee Curtis looks very different after she refused to use any "body-shapers, surgery" and "sucking in her stomach" for a new movie.

The 53-year-old took to Instagram to give her fans another side of herself.

In the snap, the actress is dressed up as her character from the film Everything Everywhere All At Once, where she plays an IRS auditor.

The photo has Jamie Lee sitting in a cubicle and eating a cookie while dressed in a tight, yellow turtleneck that shows off her tummy to go along with gray dress pants.

The movie star, who shows off her bangs, also sports glasses and what looks like a hand and wrist brace.

She captioned it: "WELL…. here we GO! Two years ago we made this movie right up until the day the world shut down.

Jamie Lee then got a bit more personal and explained why she decided to go all natural for her role.

She said: "In the world, there is an industry — a billion-dollar, trillion-dollar industry — about hiding things. Concealers. Body-shapers. Fillers. Procedures. Clothing. Hair accessories. Hair products. Everything to conceal the reality of who we are.

'YOU CAN'T GET IT BACK'

Jamie Lee being so open about her body should not come as a surprise as she has had no problem touching on the subject of plastic surgery in the past.

She once told Fast Company during an interview: "I tried plastic surgery and it didn’t work. It got me addicted to Vicodin. I’m 22 years sober now."

The famous star continued: "The current trend of fillers and procedures, and this obsession with filtering, and the things that we do to adjust our appearance on Zoom are wiping out generations of beauty. Once you mess with your face, you can’t get it back.”

'A REAL DANGER'

She also made it clear the impact social media can have one a person.

“We just don’t know the longitudinal effect, mentally, spiritually, and physically, on a generation of young people who are in agony because of social media, because of the comparisons to others," she explained.
