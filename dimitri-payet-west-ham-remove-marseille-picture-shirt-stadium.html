Dimitri Payet: West Ham remove all trace of Marseille man by getting rid of his picture and shirt fr

WEST HAM began to wipe away all traces of Dimitri Payet by removing his picture from the club’s London Stadium ground.

But the £25million France star taunted Hammers boss Slaven Bilic, saying: “I don’t have to justify anything.”

Payet’s image is being replaced by a large-scale print recreating Andy Carroll’s stunning overhead-kick goal against Crystal Palace.

And a giant West Ham shirt with Payet’s name and old 27 squad number which hangs from the ceiling inside the stadium is also being dismantled.

A Hammers source said: “We think that should come down before the fans do it for us.”

Angry supporters threw eggs at Payet’s rented Essex house after he downed tools in a bombshell meeting with boss Bilic when the club was struggling near the bottom of the Premier League.

They have also sung anti-Payet songs and sprayed obscene slogans on walls near the club’s East London home.

Payet, 29, finally got his wish to rejoin former club Marseille although West Ham witheld his £500,000 January wages.

As he was unveiled back at Stade Velodrome, Payet said: “I don’t have to justify my behaviour. Slaven Bilic can have his opinion on what I’ve done. But we had a lot of discussions, just the two of us.

“I know what we said. What happened in the past, I will talk about when it’s needed, but not now.”
