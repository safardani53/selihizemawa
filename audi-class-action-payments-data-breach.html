One-time payment up to $5,350 from $3.5million settlement available after compromised info - see if

MILLIONS of Americans are owed direct payments ranging from $20 to $5,350.

Audi owners are able to claim from a $3.5million pot after customers’ information was allegedly stolen in a data breach.

Audi and its parent company, The Volkswagen Group, have agreed to pay out after admitting to a data breach spanning several years.

WHO IS ELIGIBLE?

The settlement benefits customers who received a notice from Audi or Volkswagen informing them that their personal information (PI) or sensitive personal information (SPI) was exposed in the breach that occurred between 2019 and 2021.

There are three subclasses of eligibility, and the size of the payment customers are due depends on which category they fall under.

Take a look below to see how much you could be owed.

Read more direct payments

  • The nationwide PI subclass is made up of class members whose PI was compromised in the breach. These members can receive payments of $20.
  • The nationwide SPI subclass consists of class members whose SPI was compromised. These members can receive payments of $80.
  • A third category - the California SPI subclass - is made up of members whose SPI was compromised while they were residing in California. These members are due $350.

However, actual payment amounts may be higher or lower depending on the number of claimants who come forward during the settlement.

HUGE PAYOUTS

If you fall under the nationwide SPI subclass or the California SPI subclass, you could be entitled to considerably more.

The settlement is offering reimbursement for out-of-pocket expenses related to the data breach.

Eligible claimants can also file for up to $5,000 for fraud, identity theft, credit expenses, communication charges, and up to four hours of unpaid time off work at $50 per hour.

This means customers in California could have as much as $5,350 coming their way.

If you believe you are eligible, you can file a claim now.

To receive a settlement, you must submit a valid claim form by April 12, 2023.

The final approval hearing for the settlement is scheduled for May 18, 2023.

WHY THE PAYOUT?

The settlement comes after consumers took action against Audi and Volkswagen, who announced a data breach in 2021 that affected over 3.3million customers.

The breach reportedly exposed sales and marketing data from 2014 to 2019 and occurred between August 2019 and May 2021.

Although the brands have not admitted any wrongdoing, they agreed to shell out $3.5million in a class action settlement to resolve the allegations.

MORE MONEY

Audi isn’t the only company issuing money back to its customers.

Keurig is dishing out a total of $10million after claims it misled consumers about its K Cups being recyclable.

Those who bought Keurig K Cups to make their coffee in the last six years may get their money back.

If you purchased K Cup single-serving coffee pods between June 8, 2016 and August 8, 2022, that were labeled as recyclable in the U.S., you qualify for a refund.

There is no proof of purchase required, but it will determine how much money you get, ranging from $5 to $36.
