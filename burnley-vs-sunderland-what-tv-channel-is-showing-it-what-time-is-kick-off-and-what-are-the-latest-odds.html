Burnley vs Sunderland: What TV channel is showing it, what time is kick-off and what are the latest

AFTER a dour goalless draw in the FA Cup third round first-leg at the Stadium of Light, the Premier League duo will go head-to-head for a spot in the fourth round.

Burnley will be hoping for a repeat performance last time they hosted David Moyes' Sunderland when they ran out 4-1 winners.

The Clarets head into the fixture as favourites and with Sunderland's injury crisis, they will certainly fancy their chances.

A home tie with either Fleetwood or Bristol City awaits the winner.

When is Burnley vs Sunderland?

The FA Cup replay kicks off on Tuesday evening at 19:45.

Where can I watch the game?

The clash isn't being shown live on TV but you can follow all the action with SunSport's dedicated Live Blog.

What is the latest Burnley team news?

Sean Dyche may rest a number of players following the impressive 1-0 victory over Southampton on Saturday.

Joey Barton may start after his goal as he looks to improve on his match fitness. John Flanagan is also pushing for a rare start.

Who is definitely out for Burnley?

Scott Arfield and Joey Gudmundsson are both ruled out with hamstring injuries although they are nearing a return.

What is the latest Sunderland team news?

David Moyes has a big injury crisis ahead of the FA Cup clash with eight key members missing not including those at the Africa Cup of Nations.

The Scot didn't use any of his substitutes in the 3-1 defeat to Stoke as he named a bench full of youth players.

Javier Manquillo could replace Love at right-back.

Who is definitely out for Sunderland?

Steven Pienaar, Victor Anichebe, Jordan Pickford, Jan Kirchoff, Duncan Watmore, Lyndon Gooch, Paddy McNair and Lee Cattermole are all injured.

Wahbi Kazhri, Lamine Kone and Didier Ndong are all at the Africa Cup of Nations.
