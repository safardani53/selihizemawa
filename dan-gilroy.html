Dan Gilroy Biography

A native of Santa Monica, California, Dan Gilroy was born on His father Frank D. Gilroy is a Pulitzer Prize-winning playwright and mother Ruth Dorothy is a sculptor and writer. He was raised alongside his brothers Tony Gilroy who is now a screenwriter and director and Fraternal twin brother, John Gilroy, a film editor. Dan Gilroy is an American and belongs to white ethnicity.

Talking about his education, Gilroy studied at Washingtonville High School and later he graduated from Dartmouth College.

Since Gilroy has been active in the entertainment industry. He debuted with the film “Freejack” as a co-writer. Till the date, he has worked in 9 movies as a co-writer which includes Esq”, “Kong: Skull Island”, “The Bourne Legacy”, “Real Steel”, “The Fall”, “Two for the Money”, and “Chasers”.

However, he rose to fame only after the release of “Nightcrawler”, which is his directorial debut.
