Shocking moment Portsmouth and Plymouth football fans clash at 'dockyard derby' in 'shocking throwba

A FOOTBALL hooligan mob have been jailed after clashing with rival fans and police officers, with a judge saying their actions we a "throwback" to the dark days of the 1980s.

Thugs supporting Portsmouth Football Club ambushed Plymouth Argyle fans ahead of a League One clash between the two sides last year.

They mob then clashed with police officers before charging around 80 Plymouth supporters as they left a train station in Portsmouth, Hants.

During the exchange they hurled coins, bottles and lighters at them.

A court heard one terrified Plymouth fan was repeatedly booted in the head while on the floor, while a police officer was also assaulted as he tried to arrest someone.

The violence was so bad, experienced police superintendent Kelly Whiting revealed she had been "frightened" watching it unfold.

Jailing eight of the 16 thugs involved in the rampage, Judge Timothy Mousley QC said the gang turned up at the train station at 8.30am on match day with a "clear intent to fight".

The judge added the "prolonged and premeditated" violence had been a "throwback" to the height of football hooliganism in 1970s and 1980s.

Portsmouth Crown Court heard more than 100 extra police officers had to be drafted in at the last minute in a bid to stop the rampage happening.

It was revealed the extra policing cost the taxpayer £43,000 and Pompey £13,000.

Prosecutor Simon Jones told the court there were a number of clashes on April 14 last year before kick off in the "dockyard derby", which ended in a 1-1 draw.

He said: "There were surges of Portsmouth fans trying to get to Plymouth fans. Police were trying to keep fans apart to prevent violence."

Shocking footage played in court showed police charging into a group of fighting supporters with batons, as one man in a green hoodie was kicked as he lay on the floor.

Other video footage showed officers trying to hold back a large group of raging fans who are attempted to bait opposition supporters.

The court heard Robbie Fowler, 22, who was serving a four-year ban from attending matches at the time, was the "most prominent" of the thugs, with him even kicking out at a police dog and trying to get an officer to fight him.

Mr Jones told the court a second thug, 33-year-old Matthew Allinson, was threw "missiles" in the direction of away fans, while 20-year-old Ryan Keating had kicked a Plymouth fan who had been knocked to the ground.

It was heard others involved in the rampage included Richard Hampshire, 26, Anthony Hopkins, 22, Tommy Russell, 20, Harry Jarvie, 21, Jack Stobart, Asa Palmer, both 23, Simon Hore, 33, and 26-year-old Shane Bartram.

Also in the dock were Sean Mitchell, 46, and 19-year-olds Ryan Keating, Harley Hawkins, Louis Glasspool, Connor Bowen and Tommy Houlden.

Fowler, Houlden, Stobart and Bartram were all convicted of violent disorder, while the remaining 12 admitted one count each of the same charge.

Judge Mousley jailed Fowler for two years, Allinson for 18 months, Hampshire and Mitchell for 14 months and Keating for 13 months.

Hopkins, Russell and Hawkins were each handed 12 month jail terms. All eight were banned from attending games for six years.

The judge said: "This did not arise out of a sporting rivalry but from a clear intent to fight in public on a large scale.

"Had it not been for the police there would have been a flashpoint involving about 200 men which I have no doubt was the intention on both sides.

"There were no serious injuries but clearly there was the potential for them but for the well organised and professional response of the police.

"It is a matter of luck there were no serious injuries especially to the man on the floor getting kicked."

The judge handed Hore a 13-month jail term, Jarvie, Bartram and Stobart 12 months in custody and Palmer nine months, all suspended for 18 months.

Glasspool was sentenced to 10 months in a young offenders institute and Bowen eight months, while Houlden received 15 months behind bars, all suspended for 18 months.

Senior CPS prosecutor Gavin Sumpter said after the sentencing: "Violence and disorder in football will not be tolerated and we hope this case will be a deterrent to other like minded individuals."

Hampshire police superintendent Kelly Whiting said: "These are people who have been causing problems at Portsmouth Football Club for years and since we’ve taken them out there has been far less disorder.

"There were families and children walking around Guildhall Square where most of this trouble happened.

"I was frightened to watch it all – and I was only sat in the control room."
