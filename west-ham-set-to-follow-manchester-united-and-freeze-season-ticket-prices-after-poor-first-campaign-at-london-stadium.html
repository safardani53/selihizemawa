West Ham to follow Manchester United and freeze season ticket prices after poor first campaign at Lo

WEST Ham are set to freeze season ticket prices after a disastrous first campaign at the London Stadium, SunSport understands.

The Hammers - who drew 2-2 at home to West Brom on Saturday - are close to announcing their renewal prices for next season.

And it is believed they will NOT be increasing 2017-18 season ticket costs because of the mega-money £5.14billion TV deal.

West Ham have also had a poor first campaign in their new stadium, winning just five league matches out of 13 so far.

A Hammers source said: "It would come as a big shock if the situation was to change. It's a big issue for fans.

"Talks at board level have already began on the prices. Things are more than likely going to remain as they are.

"Most of the board are leaning this way and a formal announcement is not far away at all."

West Ham have the cheapest adult season ticket in the Premier League at just £289.

More on West Ham

An under-16s ticket costs just £99 and a family of four can purchase one for a combined £776.

Manchester United revealed they would be freezing their ticket costs last week for the sixth season in a row.

It was the seventh time in eight years that the Red Devils have decided against the increase.

Everton have also taken the same stance and other Premier League clubs are set to follow.

Supporters groups have called for season tickets to be frozen by all clubs for the entire three-year contract of the new TV deal.

A cap on Premier League away tickets was agreed at the beginning of this campaign.
