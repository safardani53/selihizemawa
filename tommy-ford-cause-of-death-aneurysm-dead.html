“An aneurysm is congenital sometimes,” Dr. Carol Richardson-Te, who has not treated Tommy, tells HollywoodLife.com EXCLUSIVELY. That means it’s possible Tommy’s aneurysm, which lead to his death, could have been there since birth. Unfortunately congenital aneurysms can go completely undiagnosed. “It is something that just happens, you could have a malformation of an artery. These things are asymptomatic and they don’t present themselves with anything until you find them, because you are looking for something else; Or for some reason, something in that area causes you to look under imaging and you find it.” This is so sad.

A lot of the time there are no symptoms or warning signs of aneurysms, so doctors could very well miss them if they aren’t directly looking in certain areas. “When do we do a brain imaging? — During trauma or with unexplained headaches, altered mental status and things like that,” Dr. Richardson-Te said, emphasizing that most of the time they go unnoticed because specific testing only happens during select medical needs.

However, please note this is not Tommy’s official or confirmed cause of death.

Tommy underwent knee replacement surgery Sept. 28, barely a week before his untimely passing. A lot of concerned fans were wondering if his surgery had something to do with his death. Like Dr. Richardson-Te EXCLUSIVELY told us, “A knee surgery would not cause an aneurysm. The aneurysm is totally unrelated.”
