Mae Jemison (Astronaut) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Mae Carol Jemison is an American engineer, doctor and former NASA astronaut. She became the first black woman to travel to space while working as a mission specialist on the space shuttle Endeavor. Jemison joined the NASA Astronaut Army in 1987 and was selected to serve on the STS-47 mission, in which it orbited Earth for nearly eight days between September 12 and 20, 1992. Born in Alabama and raised in Chicago, Jemison graduated from Stanford University with a degree in chemical engineering as well as African-American and African-American research. She then earned her medical degree at Cornell University. Jemison was a doctor for the Peace Corps in Liberia and Sierra Leone from 1983 to 1985 and a general practitioner. To become an astronaut, she applied to NASA. Jemison left NASA in 1993 and founded a technology research company.
