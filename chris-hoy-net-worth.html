Chris Hoy, a former track cyclist and Racing driver from Scotland, has a net worth of $50 million and has represented Scotland at the commonwealth games and the UK at the Olympics and world championships. 

Hoy has won the world championship eleven times and the Olympic title six times. The athlete is the second-most decorated Olympic cyclist of all time with seven medals, including six gold and one silver.

Chris bagged three gold medals at the 2008 Summer Olympics, making him Scotland’s most successful Olympian and the first British sportsperson to do so since Henry Taylor in 1908. 

Speaking with the PA news agency, the former track cyclist said the United Kingdom’s track cyclists could be on the right track to dominate at another Olympics Games after their success in last month’s world championships. 

He added, “they’ve got the quality to be very optimistic.” “Britain can be the in the mix in the majority of the events with a good chance of winning all of them.”

Brief Glance At Chris Hoy

Sir Christopher Andrew Hoy, popularly known as Chris Hoy, was born on 23 March 1976 in Edinburgh, Scotland. As a fun fact, the 1982 film E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial inspired him to start riding bicycles at six.

Hoy raced BMX between 7 and 14, ranking him second in the United Kingdom, fifth in Europe, and ninth in the World. With his fantastic talent, Chris even received sponsorships from Slazenger and Kwik-fit.  

He studied at Georgia Waston’s College and later joined the University of Edinburgh, where he studied Mathematics and Physics until 1996. 

The athlete also represented the Scotland Junior Rowing Team and came second in the 1993 National Rowing Championships. 

Throughout his career, Hoy won 11 world championships, six Olympic champions, and two Commonwealth games. He was awarded BBC sports personality of the year in 2008. 

Chris was listed in the 2009 University of Edinburgh’s Sports Hall of Fame as an honor and tribute to his works. His Velodrome was established in 2012 for the Glasgow 2014 Commonwealth Games.

The train operating company SouthEastern also featured a train under his name Class395.

Some of the honors under his name are highlighted below.

  • Sportsman of the Year, by the Sports Journalists Association in 2008
  • BBC Sports Personality of the Year (2008)
  • Honorary Doctor of Science (2009)
  • Honorary Group Captain RAFAC, Ambassador to the Royal Air Force Cadets (2013)

Tokyo 2020 Olympics

Since Sir Chris Hoy is retired, he will not participate in the 2020 Tokyo Olympics. However, his former teammates are going to Tokyo, and he is very excited about that.

Hoy is Britain’s most decorated Olympian and has won six gold medals. His former teammate and good friend Jason Kenny has already matched that record. 

Now, Kenny only requires one gold medal to be the most decorated Olympian in Britain, and Chris cannot contain his excitement about that. Moreover, he is cheering on his friend to surpass his record. 

Likewise, Jason’s wife, Laura Kenny, is also a four-time Olympic gold medalist who is going to Tokyo. Hoy recently said that Laura is an equally great athlete, if not better, and has the potential to win three gold medals and be the first to surpass his record. 

Nonetheless, the former cyclist is rooting for both to do exceptionally well in the Olympics and make the country proud. Besides that, he hopes that all the athletes participating in the Olympics make the best out of it. 

Furthermore, he added that, although this might not be ideal for first-time Olympians, it is a great success that the competition is even happening considering the circumstances. 

Hence, he encouraged participants to go there with the same enthusiasm and spirit even though no fans would cheer them on.
