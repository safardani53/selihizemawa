Career Earnings &amp; Retirement Hints

Born on December 1, 1986, in Los Angeles, California, DeSean William Jackson is the son of Bill and Gayle Jackson.

He honed his skills at Long Beach Polytechnic High School, emerging as one of the nation’s most highly regarded wide receiver recruits.

Jackson’s outstanding talent earned him the Glenn Davis Award in 2004, presented by the Los Angeles Times as Southern California’s player of the year.

Additionally, he was recognized as the Most Valuable Player in the U.S. Army All-American Bowl and awarded the title of 2004 Mr. Football State Player of the Year.

Retirement Hints

Considered one of the most formidable deep threats in NFL history, the illustrious career of DeSean Jackson may be approaching its end.

In a recent Instagram post, the free agent wide receiver shared a reflective message, stating, “Did it my way 15 years strong!! Neva anotha like it!”

While he has not yet made an official retirement announcement, the sentiment expressed in his post strongly hints at such a possibility in the near future.

Last year, at 36, Jackson contemplated retirement before ultimately joining the Baltimore Ravens in October to address their need for a wide receiver.

Despite limited playing time, his remarkable 17.0 yards-per-catch average demonstrated his enduring ability to outpace defenses and deliver deep-threat impact when called upon.

Career Earnings

DeSean Jackson secured a lucrative five-year contract with the Philadelphia Eagles in 2012, valued at an impressive $47 million.

In 2014, he inked a three-year deal with the Washington Redskins, worth $24 million.

His subsequent move to the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 2017 brought him a substantial three-year contract worth $33.5 million, featuring a guaranteed sum of $20 million.

Throughout his illustrious NFL career, DeSean Jackson’s salary earnings alone amounted to nearly $92 million.

Controversy

Over the years, DeSean Jackson’s playstyle has drawn negative media attention due to instances of poor judgment, particularly involving premature celebrations that have resulted in fumbles and lost touchdowns.

In 2020, Jackson stirred controversy when he shared an Instagram post featuring a quote falsely attributed to Adolf Hitler, along with the phrase “Hitler was right.”

He also posted quotes from antisemitic extremist Louis Farrakhan, which he later deleted and expressed remorse for.

In an effort to rectify his actions, Jackson agreed to engage in conversations with prominent members of the Philadelphia Jewish community.

He had meaningful discussions with Holocaust survivor Edward Mosberg and even visited the Auschwitz-Birkenau State Museum, demonstrating a commitment to understanding the gravity of his actions and seeking education and reconciliation.
